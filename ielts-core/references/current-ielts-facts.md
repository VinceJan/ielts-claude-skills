## Test Format

- IELTS has Listening, Reading, Writing, and Speaking.
- Academic and General Training share Listening and Speaking.
- Academic and General Training differ in Reading and Writing.
- Listening: about 30 minutes, 4 parts, 40 questions.
- Reading: 60 minutes, 3 sections/passages, 40 questions.
- Writing: 60 minutes, 2 tasks. Task 2 carries more weight than Task 1.
- Speaking: 11-14 minutes, 3 parts, recorded interview with an examiner.

## Paper vs Computer Timing

- Paper Listening normally includes about 10 minutes to transfer answers.
- Computer-delivered Listening timing can differ; do not promise the same transfer time.
- Reading has no extra transfer time; answers must be completed within 60 minutes.

## Scoring

- Listening and Reading each have 40 questions. Each correct answer earns 1 raw mark.
- Raw Listening/Reading scores are converted to whole or half IELTS bands.
- Official guidance says exact raw-score boundaries can vary slightly by test version.
- Use raw-score tables as practice approximations, not guaranteed official conversions.
- Writing is assessed on four criteria: Task Achievement/Task Response, Coherence and Cohesion, Lexical Resource, Grammatical Range and Accuracy.
- Speaking is assessed on four criteria: Fluency and Coherence, Lexical Resource, Grammatical Range and Accuracy, Pronunciation.
- Speaking criteria are equally weighted. Writing tasks are separate; Task 2 carries more weight than Task 1.
- Overall band score is the average of the four section bands, rounded to the nearest whole or half band. Averages ending in .25 round up to the next half band; averages ending in .75 round up to the next whole band.

## AI Scoring Calibration

- Never present an AI score as an official IELTS score.
- Give a likely band range when evidence is thin.
- For Writing/Speaking, explain the strongest evidence for each criterion and the main uncertainty.
- Recommend periodic calibration with official sample answers, examiner comments, mock exams, or multiple independent scoring tools.
